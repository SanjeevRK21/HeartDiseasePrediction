Heart Diseases & Their Risk of Heart Attack

Here’s a breakdown of heart diseases and how likely they are to cause a heart attack.

❤️ Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Blocked or narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to the heart.
Risk: 80-90%

🩸 Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Increases strain on heart arteries, leading to plaque buildup and rupture.
Risk: 50-70%

💔 Atherosclerosis

Hardening & narrowing of arteries due to cholesterol buildup.
Risk: 50-75%

⚡ Arrhythmias (Irregular Heartbeats)

Unstable heart rhythms can lead to cardiac arrest & heart attack.
Risk: 20-40%

🫀 Cardiomyopathy

Heart muscle disease weakens the heart’s ability to pump blood.
Risk: 20-35%

🛑 Heart Valve Disease

Faulty heart valves disrupt normal blood flow, causing strain.
Risk: 10-25%

📊 Congenital Heart Disease

Birth defects affecting heart structure can increase attack risk.
Risk: 10-20%

⛵ Diabetes-Related Heart Disease

High blood sugar damages arteries, increasing heart attack chances.
Risk: 30-50%

😢 Obesity-Related Heart Disease

Excess fat leads to high cholesterol & blood pressure issues.
Risk: 30-50%

How to Keep Your Heart Healthy ❤️

Follow these simple steps to maintain a healthy heart.

🥗 Eat Healthy

Include fruits, veggies, whole grains, and lean proteins in your diet.

🏃 Stay Active

Exercise at least 30 minutes a day to keep your heart strong.

⚖️ Maintain a Healthy Weight

Control your weight to reduce stress on your heart.

🩺 Check Blood Pressure

Monitor blood pressure and cholesterol regularly.

🧘 Manage Stress

Practice meditation and deep breathing to reduce stress.

🚭 Avoid Smoking

Quit smoking to improve heart and lung health.

💧 Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to maintain circulation.

😴 Get Enough Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep to reduce heart disease risk.

😄 Stay Happy

Laughter and social connections help keep your heart strong.
